# SRD 5.2.1 Rule Implementation — Detailed Spec

All D&D 5e SRD 5.2.1 mechanics — movement, combat, action economy, checks, saves, conditions, classes, spells, monsters — plus the engine systems (event bus, triggers, NPC AI, factions) that let those rules play out automatically without GM intervention.


- **US-001** As a player, I want to move my character around the world using keyboard input (WASD and arrow keys) so that I can explore the game world. The world is represented as a tile-based map where each tile equals 5 feet, making movement structured and tile-aligned.
- **US-005** As a player, I want to engage in turn-based combat with enemies so that exploration feels challenging and rewarding. Combat is fully integrated into the map: movement (30 ft = 6 tiles per turn) and actions happen on the same map, following SRD 5.2.1 rules (initiative, attack rolls, weapon properties and masteries, class features, monster traits, death saving throws). A Critical Hit rolls all damage dice twice including any rider dice (e.g. Sneak Attack); the crit floor defaults to natural 20 but is widened to 19-20 by **Improved Critical** (Champion Fighter L3) and 18-20 by **Superior Critical** (Champion L15) — see US-091. Sneak Attack itself is gated by the SRD — Finesse or Ranged weapon, once per turn, and either Advantage on the attack roll OR an unincapacitated ally within 5 ft of the target while the Rogue is not at Disadvantage; parried hits don't burn the once-per-turn (see US-091). Making a ranged attack (including thrown weapons) while within 5 feet of an enemy who can see the attacker and does not have the Incapacitated condition imposes Disadvantage on the attack roll. Death Saving Throws follow the full SRD rules: rolling a natural 1 counts as two failures; rolling a natural 20 immediately restores 1 HP and ends the Unconscious condition; taking any damage while at 0 HP causes one automatic failure (two if the damage is from a Critical Hit); accumulating 3 successes makes the character Stable, which stops further death saves without restoring HP. **At-0-HP massive damage** (SRD Damage at 0 HP rule): if any damage taken while at 0 HP equals or exceeds the player's HP maximum, the player dies instantly — this is in addition to the leftover-damage instant-death rule in US-049 that fires when the killing blow itself spills over the HP max. The event log is a two-column display: the left column shows immersive narrative ("Hit — 8 slashing"), the right column shows dice detail ("d20(14)+5=19 vs AC 14 · 1d6[5]+3"). Each row is colour-coded by outcome. It is possible to scroll through the entire event log.
- **US-017** As a developer, I want every creature's stat block to be sourced directly from the D&D 5e SRD 5.2.1 so that combat mechanics are correct and new creatures can be added without guesswork. Monsters (`MonsterDef`) hold SRD stat blocks: creature type, HP dice formula, full ability scores, an explicit `initiativeBonus` field, attack entries with attack type (melee / ranged / both), reach, and range, plus optional `resistances` / `vulnerabilities` / `immunities` / `conditionImmunities` arrays and `nimbleEscape` / similar trait flags. NPCs (`NPCDef`) are a separate type that references a `monsterClass` for their stat block while adding identity fields (name, colour, persona). The combat system selects the correct attack type based on range. New monsters drop into `server/data/monsters/` as JSON files; the count and identities are content, not requirements.
- **US-024** As a player, I want to fight multiple enemies in a single combat encounter so that battles feel more strategic. Simple Combat spawns 2–4 enemies chosen randomly from the available monster pool in `server/data/monsters/`. All map enemies join combat the moment one triggers it. Enemies and allies share a single uppercase A–Z combat-label pool (`assignCombatLabel` picks the lowest unused letter regardless of disposition), so the first enemy and the first ally in the room might be labelled "(A)" and "(B)" respectively. The label is rendered in the centre of the token during combat and in the Turn Order Bar at the top of the Game Map. All non-neutral NPCs (enemies and allies) act sequentially during the NPC phase; enemies attack the player and allies attack the nearest enemy. The active chip in the Turn Order Bar is highlighted green and dead chips are dimmed. Combat ends only when all enemy-disposition NPCs are defeated; surviving allies revert to neutral disposition.
- **US-025** As a player, I want the action economy to follow D&D 5e SRD rules so that combat feels authentic. Each player turn grants one Action, one Bonus Action, and one free object interaction. Attack consumes the Action; Second Wind (Fighter) consumes the Bonus Action; Drinking a Health Potion costs the Bonus Action in combat and is free during exploration. **Hide** costs the Action by default, but a Rogue of level 2 or higher gets **Cunning Action** which lets them spend a **Bonus Action** instead on any one of **Dash, Disengage, or Hide** (SRD Rogue L2 feature; not Hide alone). **Equipping or unequipping a weapon or shield** during `player_turn` uses the free object interaction the first time per turn; a second swap that turn requires the Utilize action and consumes the Action (server enforces; the engine refuses with an in-fiction log line when both are spent). **Equipping or unequipping armor** is blocked entirely during combat (SRD donning times are 1–10 minutes — impossible to fit into a 6-second round). The player remains in their turn after spending either resource and must press End Turn to hand initiative to the enemies. Action Buttons that require a spent resource are dimmed and disabled.
- **US-034** As a player, I want to throw inventory items as ranged attacks so that I have a ranged option when no adjacent enemy is present. Proper thrown weapons (javelin, dagger) use their weapon stats, mastery, and proficiency bonus; all other items are improvised (1d4 bludgeoning, STR mod, no proficiency bonus). Each weapon JSON carries `thrown`, `throwNormal`, and `throwLong` fields (ft); improvised range is 20/60 ft. A THROW… button appears in the Player Panel when the action has not been spent, no enemy is adjacent, and at least one throwable item can reach a living enemy; pressing it opens an inline picker listing qualifying items. Item range per-item: javelin 30/120 ft, dagger 20/60 ft, improvised 20/60 ft. On a hit, the thrown item enters the target's inventory and is dropped to the map at the target's tile when the creature is killed, allowing it to be recovered. On a miss, the item is placed directly on the map at the target's tile. The AIGM can also trigger throws (including map items) via the `throw_item` tool.
- **US-035** As a developer, I want all SRD weapon mastery properties implemented so that weapons behave correctly. All four masteries used by player weapons are now implemented: **Graze** (miss still deals damage equal to the ability modifier used to make the attack roll — STR for typical Glaive/Greatsword, DEX for Finesse weapons — of the weapon's damage type), **Vex** (hit gives the attacker Advantage on their next attack roll against that creature before the end of the attacker's next turn), **Sap** (hit gives the target Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of the attacker's next turn), **Slow** (hit reduces target speed by 10 ft until the start of the attacker's next turn). Each is resolved in `CombatSystem.ts` and wired into the event log. Additional masteries delivered by US-091: **Push** (10 ft shove on hit; SRD: only against Large-or-smaller targets) and **Topple** (Con save vs. DC = 8 + ability modifier used for the attack roll + PB or take Prone) implemented as post-hit riders on `PlayerAttack`. (The eight SRD 5.2.1 masteries are Cleave, Graze, Nick, Push, Sap, Slow, Topple, and Vex — no "Flex" mastery exists.)
- **US-036** As a player, I want saving throws tracked for all creatures so that the AIGM can call for saves when the story demands it. Every creature carries a `savingThrows` map of all six modifiers: character JSONs derive these at load time from `savingThrowProficiencies` (ability modifier + proficiency bonus if proficient); monster JSONs store the values directly from the SRD stat block SAVE column. All six player saving throws are surfaced to the AIGM on every message. The AIGM actively calls for saving throws — against traps, spells, environmental hazards, or any narrative moment where a save is appropriate — using the same `request_ability_check` flow as skill checks, rolling d20 + the relevant save modifier, then narrating success or failure and applying consequences via its other tools.
- **US-037** As a player, I want my full skill list stored and used in play so that ability checks reflect my character's actual proficiencies and create meaningful risk. Each character's `skills` map (stored in the character JSON and persisted in `SaveData`) holds all 18 SRD skills keyed by camelCase name (e.g. `sleightOfHand`). Each value is the pre-computed total modifier: ability modifier plus proficiency bonus once for proficiency, twice for expertise, or not at all if untrained. The dedicated `perceptionBonus` and `stealthBonus` fields on `PlayerDef` are replaced by the general map. Skills are surfaced to the AIGM on every message. When the player asks to do something that does not automatically succeed, the AIGM calls a `request_ability_check` tool specifying the skill, a DC (Very Easy 5 / Easy 10 / Medium 15 / Hard 20 / Very Hard 25 per SRD), and a reason. The server rolls d20 + the skill modifier and returns the result; the AIGM overlay injects it as a distinct 🎲 roll entry (green on success, red on failure) between the player's message and the GM's narrative response. The AIGM then narrates success or failure and uses its other tools to apply consequences — awarding or deducting HP, XP, or gold, moving entities, or altering the encounter — as the fiction demands.
- **US-041** As a player, I want every creature — player, allies, enemies, and NPCs — to make Opportunity Attacks when an opponent moves out of their reach, so that positioning carries meaningful risk for everyone in the encounter. When a creature uses its movement to leave the reach of an opponent that can see it, that opponent may spend its Reaction to make one melee attack. **One Reaction per round per creature**, refreshed at the start of *that creature's own turn* (not at the start of the player's turn) — so an enemy that burned its Reaction during its turn cannot react again until it acts again. **Visibility gate**: a creature cannot take an Opportunity Attack against a target that is `hidden` or `invisible` (the SRD's "a creature you can see" clause; implemented via `ConditionSystem.isVisible`). **Incapacitation gate**: a creature with the Incapacitated, Stunned, Paralyzed, or Unconscious condition cannot take Reactions at all. The four trigger paths are all wired: (a) the player OA against an enemy that moves away — surfaced via the Reaction Prompt Overlay (see US-067), never auto-fires; (b) enemy OA against the player on player movement — auto-fires; (c) ally OA against an enemy on the enemy's turn — auto-fires; (d) enemy OA against an ally on the ally's turn — auto-fires. Disengage suppresses Opportunity Attacks against the player for the turn. **Forced-movement and Teleport exclusion** (SRD `Opportunity_Attacks.md`): a creature does not provoke an Opportunity Attack when it teleports or when it is moved without using its own movement / action / Bonus Action / Reaction (e.g. a knockback from Thunderwave, a Push mastery shove, falling). The event log notes each Opportunity Attack with both the attacker and the target identified by their disambiguated combat label.
- **US-042** As a player, I want Dash, Dodge, and Disengage actions so that I have tactical options beyond attacking. **Dash**: spend Action to gain extra movement equal to Speed for the turn. **Dodge**: until start of next turn, attacks against the player have Disadvantage and the player has Advantage on DEX saves; shown in Phase Text. The Dodge benefits are lost if the dodger has the **Incapacitated** condition or if their **Speed is 0** (SRD `Dodge_[Action].md`). **Disengage**: movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn. These appear as Action Buttons in the Player Panel when the Action has not yet been spent.
- **US-043** As a developer, I want a formal Advantage/Disadvantage system so that all D20 Test modifiers are consistent and composable. Any D20 Test can be tagged with Advantage or Disadvantage sources. When resolving the roll: Advantage → roll 2d20, take higher; Disadvantage → take lower; both present → cancel, roll normally. Sources include: player hidden (Advantage on attack), Vex mastery (Advantage on attacker's next attack roll against the same target), Dodge (Disadvantage on attacks against dodger), Surprise (Disadvantage on surprised creature's Initiative), ranged attack while adjacent to a conscious enemy (Disadvantage). The event log shows both dice values when the mechanic applies.
- **US-046** As a player, I want to take a Short Rest after an encounter so that I can recover HP between fights by spending Hit Dice. A SHORT REST button appears in the Player Panel during the exploring phase when the player is below max HP and has Hit Dice remaining. Each click spends one Hit Die (d10+CON for Fighter, d8+CON for Rogue) and heals that amount, logged in the Event Log. Hit Dice equal the character level; they reset at the start of every new encounter (Long Rest equivalent).
- **US-047** As a developer, I want Passive Perception implemented so that hiding is not an automatic success. Passive Perception = 10 + Perception modifier, adjusted by **+5 if the creature has Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks** and **−5 for Disadvantage** (SRD `Passive_Perception.md`). The active-Hide check (see US-061) and the passive-Perception sweep that finds hidden NPCs (see US-086) both consult this value; failure is logged and the hider remains visible.
- **US-048** As a player, I want Surprise to apply when combat starts unexpectedly. Per SRD: Surprised creatures roll Initiative with Disadvantage; Invisible creatures roll with Advantage. The engine detects Surprise on `doStartCombat`: any enemy NPC is Surprised iff `s.player.conditions.includes('hidden')` at the moment combat triggers (i.e. the player attacked from stealth or moved within combat-trigger range while hidden). Each combatant rolls Initiative independently and the event log shows every roll, with `[SURPRISED]` / `[INVISIBLE]` notes. Allies and the player are not Surprised in player-initiated combat.
- **US-049** As a developer, I want Massive Damage / Instant Death implemented. When a hit reduces the player to 0 HP and leftover damage ≥ player's HP maximum, the player dies instantly without death saving throws. The event log notes the instant death.
- **US-051** As a developer, I want a formal conditions framework so that status effects are consistent, visible, and extensible. Conditions are tracked as a set per creature and their mechanical effects are applied at resolution time by the combat and movement systems. All condition logic lives in `server/src/engine/ConditionSystem.ts` and applies equally to all creature types — player, ally, and enemy. The Target Panel shows active conditions on the selected creature in amber. The AIGM can apply and remove conditions via `apply_condition` and `remove_condition` tools; exhaustion is set via `set_exhaustion_level`. The mechanical effects of each implemented condition are: **Blinded** — own attack rolls have Disadvantage; attack rolls against have Advantage. **Charmed** — the charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with damaging abilities or magical effects (SRD `Charmed_[Condition].md`); applied by Charm Person (see US-065) — the charmer also has Advantage on social ability checks against the charmed creature, surfaced through US-092's attitude axis. **Frightened** — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. **Grappled** — Speed 0; own attack rolls have Disadvantage **against any target other than the grappler** (SRD `Grappled_[Condition].md`); requires `sourceId` tracking — see US-058. **Incapacitated** — can't take Actions, Bonus Actions, or Reactions (prevents Opportunity Attacks); applies to all creatures. **Invisible** — own attack rolls have Advantage; attack rolls against have Disadvantage. **Paralyzed** — has the Incapacitated condition; Speed 0; attack rolls against have Advantage; melee hits from within 5 ft are Critical Hits (applies to melee and thrown attacks); auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Poisoned** — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. **Prone** — Disadvantage on own attack rolls; melee attacks against have Advantage, ranged attacks against have Disadvantage; standing up costs half Speed (round down). The engine currently **auto-stands at the start of the prone creature's turn** when there is enough movement to do so **Restrained** — Speed 0; own attack rolls have Disadvantage; attack rolls against have Advantage; Disadvantage on Dex saves. **Stunned** — has the Incapacitated condition; attack rolls against have Advantage; auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Unconscious** — has the Incapacitated condition; auto-applies Prone; Speed 0; attack rolls against have Advantage; melee hits from within 5 ft are Critical Hits; auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Exhaustion** (numeric level 0–6 per SRD `Exhaustion_[Condition].md`) — each level imposes **−2 to ability checks and saving throws** AND **reduces Speed by 5 ft × level**; **level 6 is lethal**. **Vexed** (custom) — own attack rolls have Disadvantage; applied by Vex and Sap weapon masteries; lasts until end of next turn. **Slowed** (custom) — Speed reduced by 10 ft; applied by Slow mastery; lasts until end of next turn. When any creature dies (HP reaches 0), the Hidden condition is cleared from that creature automatically — for NPCs this removes `"hidden"` from their conditions array; for the player, the hidden flag is reset to false.
- **US-053** As a developer, I want monster Resistance, Vulnerability, and Immunity modeled so that damage is applied correctly per SRD stat blocks. Each `MonsterDef` carries optional `resistances`, `vulnerabilities`, and `immunities` arrays of damage types, plus an optional `conditionImmunities` array. Player attacks check the target's lists and halve (resistant), double (vulnerable), or negate entirely (immune) damage before applying to HP, with a log entry noting the modifier. Condition immunity prevents the condition from being applied at all. Immunity takes precedence over vulnerability and resistance. Each `PlayerAttack` carries a `damageType` field sourced from the equipped weapon (or "bludgeoning" for Unarmed Strike). Resistance, vulnerability, immunity, and condition immunity are declared on each monster JSON — any monster can express any combination without code changes.
- **US-054** As a player, I want NPCs to have a **combat disposition** (ally, neutral, or enemy) so that the same entity can shift sides during a fight without losing its identity. **Disposition is the combat-only axis** — it answers "does this creature attack me, fight on my side, or stay out of the fight?" The orthogonal social axis (how the NPC feels about the party — Friendly / Indifferent / Hostile per SRD) is **Attitude**, owned by US-092; a hostile-attitude shopkeeper can still be neutral-disposition (won't draw a blade but resists persuasion), and a neutral-disposition guard may turn friendly-attitude after a successful Influence without joining combat. All creatures — enemies, allies, and social NPCs — share a single `NpcState` type differentiated by `disposition: 'ally' | 'neutral' | 'enemy'`. Enemy-disposition NPCs attack the player in combat; ally-disposition NPCs join the initiative order and automatically attack the nearest enemy on their turn; neutral-disposition NPCs stand in the world and do not participate in combat. Rendering is disposition-aware: all three dispositions show an HP bar overlaid at the top of the token circle when damaged — red for enemies, green for allies, blue-grey for neutrals. All NPCs show their name (or revealed name) just above the token. Enemy NPCs receive a `combatLabel` (A, B, C…) at spawn; ally NPCs receive a `combatLabel` from the same pool when combat starts or when their disposition changes. `combatLabel` is rendered in the centre of the token during combat and hidden while exploring. Token disposition is synced from server state on every tick. When combat ends, surviving allies revert to neutral; living enemies are removed from the map. Dead NPCs (HP = 0) remain on the map as corpses regardless of disposition: their token is rendered at 40% opacity with no HP bar and no label. Corpses are selectable — clicking one opens the Target Panel showing their stats — but they cannot act, be targeted by attacks, or trigger combat. The AIGM receives dead NPCs in a separate CORPSES section of the game state and can direct the player to search them. The AIGM can change any NPC's disposition at any time via `set_disposition`. Each NPC also carries an optional `revealedName` field set by the `reveal_npc_name` AIGM tool; once set, it replaces the generic name displayed above the token and is used by the sayto mode button in the HUD GM tab. An optional `combatPassive` boolean flag (set via `set_npc_passive`) causes an ally to skip their combat turn entirely, allowing the player to instruct allies to stand down without changing their disposition.
- **US-055** As a player, I can attack a neutral NPC outside of combat to start a fight, so that I have agency over when and against whom combat begins. Selecting a living non-ally NPC and pressing ATTACK during the exploring phase promotes that NPC to enemy disposition, triggers faction aggro, and then starts combat via a normal initiative roll. Faction aggro: every NPC carries a `factionId` (defaults to its definition id, so all instances of the same NPC type share a faction); when any NPC turns hostile all neutral NPCs sharing that `factionId` immediately join as enemies before initiative is rolled. Faction aggro fires from both player-initiated attacks and AIGM `set_disposition` calls, ensuring the mechanic is consistent regardless of how hostility starts.
- **US-060** As a developer, I want every combatant to roll Initiative independently so that turn order reflects each creature's roll, not a player-vs-enemy duel. `doStartCombat` rolls d20 + initiativeBonus for the player and every non-neutral NPC, applying Surprise/Invisible modifiers. The result of each roll is stored as `initiativeRoll` on `PlayerState` and `NpcState`. `turnOrderIds` is sorted by descending total (tiebroken by DEX mod / `initiativeBonus`) and combatants act in that exact order — including allies interleaved with enemies. Combat advances via `advanceTurn`, which iterates `turnOrderIds`, skipping dead combatants and dispatching to `enterPlayerTurn` for `'player'` or `runSingleEnemyTurn`/`runSingleAllyTurn` for NPCs. The HUD's Turn Order Bar displays the chips in initiative order — the player chip is no longer always first. The event log logs every Initiative roll with the dice detail in the right column.
- **US-061** As a player, I want Hide to follow SRD action economy. **Hide is a general SRD action available to every character** (previously the UI gated it to Rogues only — that gate has been dropped). Default cost is the **Action**; a Level 2+ Rogue uses **Cunning Action** to spend a **Bonus Action** instead. The HIDE button is visible whenever the player is not already hidden and is either in `exploring` (free, no resource cost — enables a Sneak Attack opener against currently-neutral NPCs for Rogues, or a Cast-from-cover opener for any caster) or in `player_turn` with the appropriate resource available. **SRD 5.2.1 Hide check** (`Hide_[Action].md`): to hide you make a **DC 15 Dexterity (Stealth) check** while you are **Heavily Obscured or behind Three-Quarters Cover or Total Cover** AND **out of any enemy's line of sight**; success grants the **Invisible** condition and the rolled total becomes the DC opponents must beat with an active Wisdom (Perception) check to find you. The condition ends immediately if you make a sound louder than a whisper, an enemy finds you, you make an attack roll, or you cast a spell with a Verbal component. The `hidden` condition persists across the combat-trigger boundary so a stealthed attack rolls with Advantage and (for Rogues) triggers Sneak Attack dice.
- **US-062** As a developer, I want SRD-faithful equip/unequip enforcement so that gear changes respect time and action economy. Armor cannot be equipped or unequipped during any combat phase (SRD donning times are 1–10 minutes — server returns an in-fiction refusal message). Weapons and shields use SRD's free object interaction rule: the first equip/unequip during `player_turn` is free, a second swap in the same turn consumes the Action (Utilize), and a third is blocked. The free-interaction state is tracked on `PlayerState.freeObjectInteractionUsed` and resets at the start of every player turn. Exploring-phase swaps remain free. Equip changes are blocked entirely during `enemy_turn`, `death_saves`, and `defeat`.
- **US-063** As a player, I want to use a bow with ammunition so that I have a true ranged combat option. The Shortbow (and any future ranged weapon — Longbow, Crossbows, Sling, Blowgun, Dart) has `rangeNormal` and `rangeLong` (in feet), an `ammunitionType` field, and optional `loading` and `heavy` flags. Equipping a ranged weapon makes the ATTACK button auto-route through `mainAttack.rangeNormal`: target reach widens to `floor(rangeLong/5)` tiles, attacks consume one ammunition item matching `ammunitionType` from inventory, and on every shot there is a 50% chance the round lands as a recoverable map item on the target's tile. Disadvantage is applied when: the target is beyond normal range (but within long), an enemy is adjacent to the player (and can see them and isn't Incapacitated), or the weapon is `heavy` and the player's DEX is below 13. Ammunition is its own equipment subtype (`type: "ammunition"`) — stackable in inventory, shown in the Inventory Overlay under a dedicated AMMO section with no use button. Ranged-weapon characters declare their starting bow and matching ammunition stack in `defaultEquipment` alongside any melee fallback.
- **US-065** As a player wizard, I want to cast spells from my prepared list and known cantrips so that I have a magical combat option. Spells live in `server/data/spells/` as `SpellDef` JSON files; caster characters declare `spellcastingAbility`, `defaultCantripIds`, `defaultSpellbookIds`, `defaultPreparedSpellIds`, and `defaultSpellSlots`. The Character Sheet's Spells tab lists each known spell with a per-row CAST button (visible iff the engine considers the spell castable right now) and a RITUAL CAST button (visible iff the spell has the Ritual tag and the character is in the exploring phase). Clicking CAST closes the sheet and either fires immediately (self / utility) or enters spell-targeting mode in the Player Panel (creature click for attack-roll spells, cursor click with cone / disc preview for AOE). The generic resolver in `SpellSystem.ts` branches on the spell's JSON shape: `attack: ranged-spell|melee-spell` (d20 + PB + ability mod vs AC), `attack: auto-hit` (Magic Missile dart spread), or `save: { ability, halfOnSuccess }` (each target rolls a save vs DC 8 + PB + ability mod, full or half damage). Cantrip damage scales with character level at L5/11/17. Spell slots are tracked on `PlayerState.spellSlots` indexed by level − 1, consumed on cast, refilled on Long Rest (= new encounter), and persisted to the character save. Spell save DC, attack bonus, prepared list, and remaining slots are surfaced to the AIGM on every message. Spell-slot counts and prepared-list size scale by Wizard level per the SRD table; **the L1→L4 progression, the Wizard Long Rest prep rebuild, and Arcane Recovery firing on Short Rest are delivered by US-091**. **Concentration** (SRD `Concentration.md`): when a caster takes damage while concentrating, the engine rolls a CON save at DC `min(30, max(10, ⌊damage/2⌋))` — the SRD caps the DC at 30 so a single colossal hit never makes the save effectively impossible. Failure ends the concentrated spell. Casting a new concentration spell drops any previous one. **Concentration also ends automatically when the caster has the Incapacitated condition or dies** (SRD: "Your Concentration ends if you have the Incapacitated condition or you die."). The Player Panel shows a "🌀 Concentrating: <Spell>" chip while active. **Mage Armor**: lasts until armor is donned; sets base AC to 13 + DEX via a `mageArmor` flag on `PlayerState` consumed by `computeAC`. **Shield**: prompted as a Reaction (see US-067) on any non-critical hit when the player can cast it (Shield in spellbook / prepared, L1 slot remaining, Reaction free); accepting consumes the reaction + an L1 slot and grants +5 AC retroactively against the triggering attack and until the start of the player's next turn. Whether to spend the slot when the attack would land anyway is the player's call — the prompt shows the attack roll and incoming damage for context. **Sleep**: targets in a 5-ft sphere roll WIS save; failures become Incapacitated; at the end of each affected creature's next turn they re-save, ending the spell on success or becoming Unconscious on a second failure. Spell definitions live as JSON in `server/data/spells/`; adding a new spell is a JSON drop plus, if the spell needs novel handling outside the generic resolver, a registered handler — the current spellbook is content, not a requirement. **Combined attack + save** (Ray of Sickness, Chill Touch): an `attack` spell that also carries `save + effect` rolls the save against the same target on a hit and applies the `effect.onFail` conditions. **Secondary AOE rider** (Ice Knife): an `attack` spell carrying `secondaryDamage + save + area` fires an independent save against creatures within the area around the target tile, regardless of whether the primary attack hit. **Chain on doubles** (Chromatic Orb): when two damage dice match, the spell leaps to the nearest other valid enemy within `chainOnDoubles.rangeFeet` and makes a fresh attack roll. **Thunderwave push** (`push: { feet }`): on a failed save the creature is shoved that many feet directly away from the caster, stopping at impassable terrain, other creatures, or the caster. **Color Spray** (`hpPool: { dice, sides }`): rolls the pool once at cast, sorts living creatures in the area by current HP ascending, and applies `effect.onFail` (Blinded) until the pool is exhausted. **Hideous Laughter repeat-saves**: while a creature is concentrating-bound Prone + Incapacitated by the spell, it re-saves at the end of each of its turns (regular roll, ends the spell on it on success) and every time it takes damage (Advantage on the roll). **Charm Person**: a Charmed creature can't target the player (the charmer) with attacks; the enemy AI omits the player from its candidate target list while the condition is set. **Temp HP / self-buffs** (False Life `tempHpRoll`, Longstrider `speedBonus` on PlayerState, Expeditious Retreat `expeditiousRetreat` flag granting an upfront Dash on cast plus a free Dash each turn while active, Jump `jumpMultiplier`): self-targeted spells write minimal runtime flags onto PlayerState that combat / movement code reads at turn start; concentration-gated buffs are cleared by `endConcentration` when the spell ends. **Riders**: Ray of Frost (slowed), Chill Touch (no-healing), Shocking Grasp (no-reactions) apply conditions on a hit via the data-driven `effect.onHit` descriptor (no per-spell engine branch); Ray of Enfeeblement's save-success `vexed` rider uses `effect.onSuccess` the same way. - **US-094** As a developer, I want an NPC simulation layer so that off-camera NPCs follow daily routines, react to noise and combat, and can be bound as the player's companion without the encounter author having to script every step. **Architecture**: the sim layer lives under `server/src/engine/npcSim/` and consists of three primitives — `NpcAction` (atomic per-tick step, e.g. `WalkOneTileAction`), `NpcTask` (multi-tick goal with `score(sim)` + `nextAction(sim)`), and `NpcTickRunner` (resumes the active task, scores all tasks in the registry, picks the highest, applies one action). Every decision pulls a seeded RNG from `SimRng.forNpcTick(tickId, npcId)` so the same world state + same tick id produces the same NPC behaviour byte-for-byte. **Day phase cycle**: `GameState.worldTickCount` is bumped once per real-time off-camera tick (6 s game time); `GameState.dayPhase` advances `morning → noon → evening → night` every `TICKS_PER_DAY_PHASE` ticks (60 by default ≈ 6 real minutes per phase). **Routines**: any `NPCDef` may declare `routine: RoutineEntry[]` with one entry per phase. Today's authored task kinds are `walk_to { tileX, tileY }` and `idle`; `RoutineRegistry.tasksForRoutine(npc, dayPhase)` translates the active row into a live task and includes `IdleTask`, `InvestigateTask`, and `AlertTask` as fallback/awareness tasks in the pool. Per-encounter scope today (each encounter starts at `morning`); cross-encounter persistence is the WorldState refactor's job (see scaffold in `server/src/engine/npcSim/WorldStateScaffold.ts`). **Companions**: `NpcState.companion` carries a `followMode` (`'follow' | 'wait'`), an optional player `override` (`attack { targetId }` consumed by `NpcTurnRunners` in combat), and the NPC's `simState`. The trigger action `set_npc_companion { defId, isCompanion, followMode? }` and the AIGM tool of the same name promote / demote any NPC; promoted NPCs run the `FollowPlayerTask` / `WaitHereTask` / `IdleTask` pool while the player drives the COMPANION chip on the Player Panel. **Awareness ladder + propagation**: every NPC carries an `alertness: 'calm' | 'suspicious' | 'alert'` value (default `calm`) and an `NpcMemory` block (`lastAlertTick`, `lastAlertTile`, `lastAlertSource`, `lastAlertKind`). Two propagation surfaces wake NPCs up: `pingFactionAlert(ctx, sourceTile, factionId, …)` is called from `doStartCombat` so every same-faction NPC within `FACTION_ALERT_RADIUS = 30` tiles flips to `alert` the moment combat begins, even if they're not in line of sight; `registerAwarenessHooks(ctx)` subscribes to the existing `noise` bus event from `Sound.ts` so every noise (combat swing, spell verbal component, footstep) raises NPCs within the source's `intensity` tile radius to `suspicious` — unifying with the Hide/perception pipeline rather than running a parallel call site. Both surfaces only ever **raise** alertness; `decayAlertness` in `WorldTick` steps the ladder back down (`alert → suspicious` after `ALERT_DECAY_TICKS.alert = 15` ticks, `suspicious → calm` after `ALERT_DECAY_TICKS.suspicious = 25` ticks) and clears memory on return to `calm`. **Awareness is movement-only**: `AlertTask` (priority `critical`) and `InvestigateTask` (priority `normal`, tolerance Chebyshev ≤ 1) walk the NPC toward `lastAlertTile`, but neither flips disposition — a neutral disguised-faction member who gets pinged walks to the source tile and stops. Threat conversion remains the faction matrix's job (`isHostileTo`) and the world-tick's `anyHostileToParty` check. **Target Panel chip**: the right-edge Target Panel shows an `ALERTNESS` row beneath the conditions row, hidden when `calm`, orange chip for `suspicious`, red chip for `alert`. **Tick eligibility**: the sim only ticks during `exploring` phase; combat freezes routine + ambient NPCs and routes companion behaviour through the existing ally AI in `NpcTurnRunners`. **Determinism contract**: given the same world state + same task registry + same tick id, every NPC's decision is reproducible — the foundation for future replay tests + the LOD pass scaffolded in `WorldStateScaffold.ts` (LOD tiers `near` / `region` / `dormant`, persistent NPC registry, four-phase migration plan).
- **US-092** As a player, I want every NPC to carry a **social Attitude** (Friendly / Indifferent / Hostile) **parallel to but distinct from combat Disposition** (US-054), so that social interactions and ability checks reflect how a creature feels about me without being conflated with whether they're currently in combat with me. **Attitude is the SRD social axis** (`Attitude.md`, `Friendly_[Attitude].md`, `Indifferent_[Attitude].md`, `Hostile_[Attitude].md`); it answers "how does this NPC feel about the party?" while `disposition` answers "is this NPC fighting me right now?" The two are **orthogonal**: a hostile-attitude shopkeeper can be neutral-disposition (won't draw a blade but resists persuasion); a neutral-disposition guard may shift to friendly-attitude after a successful Influence without joining combat; an ally-disposition mercenary keeps an indifferent attitude unless explicitly befriended. **Data shape**: every `NPCDef` JSON may declare an optional `attitude: 'friendly' | 'indifferent' | 'hostile'` field (default `'indifferent'`, matching SRD's "Indifferent is the default attitude of a monster"). `NpcState` carries the live `attitude` value, set from `NPCDef.attitude` at spawn time and mutable thereafter via tool / trigger. **Persistence**: attitude rides along the existing world save and adventure save, so a shopkeeper befriended in chapter 1 stays friendly in chapter 3. **AIGM integration**: attitude is surfaced to the GM in CURRENT STATE on every living NPC entry (e.g. `Aldric (npc_aldric) — neutral disposition · friendly attitude · HP 12/12`); the GM tool `set_attitude(entity, attitude, reason)` lets the GM shift attitude during play (e.g. on a successful Persuasion); `adjust_npc_hp`, `set_disposition`, and `set_attitude` are independent — changing one does not auto-change the other. The corresponding `TriggerAction` `set_npc_attitude { defId, attitude }` lets encounters author scripted attitude shifts. **Mechanical effect on Influence checks** (SRD `Influence_[Action].md` + the Friendly/Hostile attitude entries): a Friendly creature grants the player **Advantage** on Influence-type ability checks (Charisma Deception / Intimidation / Performance / Persuasion / Wisdom Animal Handling) against it; a Hostile creature imposes **Disadvantage**; an Indifferent creature uses the normal roll. The engine's `request_ability_check` flow accepts an optional `targetNpcId` parameter; when the requested skill is on the Influence skill list AND `targetNpcId` is set, the d20 is rolled with attitude-derived Advantage/Disadvantage via the existing US-043 system. **Charm Person now sets attitude too**: when Charm Person (US-065) lands, the engine sets the charmer's attitude on the charmed creature to `'friendly'` for the spell's duration, satisfying the SRD's "Social Advantage" branch of the Charmed condition; on spell end, attitude reverts to its pre-cast value. **Out of scope for this story**: the full Influence action button in the Player Panel (lives in US-057) and the Willing / Unwilling / Hesitant front-gate that decides whether a check is even necessary. This story delivers the data layer + AIGM tool + Charm-Person hook + the Advantage/Disadvantage wiring on the ability check resolver.

- **US-095** As a player, I want concealed traps and deployable area-denial gear so that stealth, gadgetry, and clever use of equipment are first-class tactical options. **Concealed traps** are a new entity (`GameState.traps: TrapState[]`) authored per-encounter via `EncounterDef.traps` (`EncounterTrapDef`) and instantiated by `SessionBuilder`. Each trap occupies a tile and is **detected**, **disarmed**, or **sprung**: (a) **detect** — passively as the player moves within ~10 ft when their **passive Perception ≥ `detectDC`** (mirrors the hidden-NPC sweep in `runPassiveTrapDetection`), or actively via the **Search** action (the single Wisdom (Perception) roll also probes adjacent concealed traps); (b) **disarm** — a new **DISARM TRAP** Player-Panel button rolls **Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) vs `disarmDC`** (SRD default 15, per `Tools.md` / Rogue L1), with **Advantage when the player carries Thieves' Tools** (`thieves_tools` in inventory); a botch (miss by 5+) springs the trap; (c) **spring** — stepping onto an armed trap (`ExplorationActions.doMove` → `checkTrapTriggers`) rolls `trigger.saveAbility` (default DEX) vs `trigger.saveDC`, dealing `damageDice`d`damageSides`+`damageBonus` (half on a successful save when `halfOnSave`), then applies an optional `condition` (restrained, poisoned, …); the trap is then spent. Disarmed/sprung traps render dim; discovered armed traps render a ⚠ hazard marker (`GameScene.drawTraps`) and surface in tile-info `effects`. **Area-denial gear** (caltrops, ball bearings) is the player's own trap-setting: a `GearDef.areaDenial` block turns the item into a deployable hazard. A **SET <gear>** button enters a tile-targeting mode; on placement (`TrapSystem.doDeployGear`) the gear is consumed and a persistent **`ActiveZone`** is created — the **same primitive spells use, so it renders like a spell effect and appears in tile info**. A creature entering the zone rolls the SRD enter-save (`ActiveZone.enterSave`): **caltrops** — DC 15 DEX or 1 Piercing (`ActiveZone.enterDamage`) + Speed 0 via the new turn-scoped **`hobbled`** condition (SPEED_ZERO + TURN_CONDITIONS, clears at the start of the creature's next turn); **ball bearings** — DC 10 DEX or Prone. The save resolves for the player (`tickZoneEnterSaves`) and NPCs/enemies (`applyZoneStepEffects`) alike. `AvailableActions` gains `disarmableTrapTiles` + `deployableGearIds`, gated by reach and action economy (both Disarm and Deploy cost the Action in combat). All trap + zone state persists in `world.json`. SRD adventuring-gear data objects ship for ball bearings, caltrops, crowbar, rope, and hooded lantern, plus a `thieves_tools` item. **Shipped content**: the four-chapter adventure **The Moon's Ledger** (`adventures/the_moons_ledger.json`) for **Wren Allowyn** (Halfling Rogue) teaches one mechanic per chapter — stealth/ambush, detect/disarm, area-denial gear — and combines all three in the finale (a hidden enforcer, a trapped ledger case, room to seed hazards); a standalone demo encounter **The Smuggler's Cache** is also available from the Single Encounter menu.
