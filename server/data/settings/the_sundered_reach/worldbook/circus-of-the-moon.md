The Circus of the Moon is a travelling amusement that opens only at night. Its halfling performers are genuine artists — acrobats, musicians, fire-dancers, illusionists of the wholly mundane kind — and the spectacle they put on is the best in the Empire, drawing crowds in every town the caravan visits. Beyond the travelling show, the Circus maintains permanent venues in the great cities — opera houses, dance halls, song-stages, concert rooms — staffed by artists of the first rank, so that the Circus is as much a fixture of imperial high culture as it is a wandering wonder of the roads. To the people of the Empire, the Circus is joy itself: the thing you save coin to see, the night you remember, the song your children sing for weeks after.

That is the first layer, and it is a cover. The Circus's performers are also the Empire's finest assassins, and both the caravan and the standing venues serve as their bases: a body of nimble, lethal, perfectly-trained halflings who are welcomed through any gate because they bring a night of wonder with them, and who can leave the morning after a death with the whole company as their alibi. The acrobatics that delight a crowd at dusk are the same skills that put someone on a high windowsill at midnight.

But assassination is not the bottom of the Circus either. Beneath the artistry and beneath the murder lies the faction's true purpose: the Circus of the Moon are the masters of propaganda, and they control the hearts of the people. Every other thing they do serves this. The art is not merely a cover for the killing; the art is the _instrument_, the means by which a faction reaches into the chest of an entire population and decides what it will love, fear, mourn, and cheer. A song repeated in a thousand taverns can do what no army can. A play that makes a hero of one man and a fool of another can settle a succession without a single blade drawn. A festival staged at the right moment can turn grief into loyalty, or fear into gratitude, in the space of a single night. The Circus knows the exact weight of a melody, the precise turn of a story that lodges in the mind and will not leave, and they deploy these with the cold precision of engineers. Assassination, when it is needed, is simply the bluntest tool in a kit that is otherwise made entirely of feeling.

And the hearts they steer are steered, above all, for the Empire. The Circus of the Moon is the New Empire's unofficial ministry of morale — never named as such, never chartered, never admitted, but understood. When the ashen news of a fallen holdfast must not become panic, it is the Circus that turns the story into one of sacrifice and resolve. When a [[bureau-of-adjustment|Bureau]] decree would be hated, it is the Circus that has already, weeks earlier, seeded the songs and plays that make the people receive it as wisdom. When the [[white-capes|White Capes]] do something monstrous and necessary, it is the Circus that ensures the people remember a different, gentler version by the following spring. The Empire keeps its order through the Bureau's law, the Capes' fear, and the Circus's love — and of the three, the love is the cheapest to maintain and the hardest to revolt against, because a population that has been taught what to feel does not know it is being governed at all.

This makes the Circus quietly the most insidious of the three halfling Powers. The [[silver-service|Silver Service]] buys secrets and the [[fortune-guards|Fortune Guards]] own debts, but the Circus owns the mood of the realm itself. The Service often brokers the Circus's contracts and the Circus enforces the Service's deals — under its tents, in the shadows behind its opera boxes — but the Circus's grandest work is never a contract at all. It is the slow, invisible, year-on-year shaping of what an entire people believes about itself, performed in plain sight, applauded by the very crowds it is remaking. The most dangerous thing the Circus of the Moon ever does, it does to thunderous applause.

Like its siblings, the Circus is bound by the [[oath-of-non-competition|Oath of Non-Competition]] — it keeps to art, broadly defined, and does not broker the deals it enforces. See also the [[halfling-sixer|Halfling Sixer]] — the common proverb that names the three Powers' joint method of charming a mark into ruin.
