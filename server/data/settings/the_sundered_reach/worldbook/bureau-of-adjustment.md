The Bureau of Adjustment is the legislative body of the Empire and the authority over all imperial law and the regular military. Where the White Capes own magic, the Bureau owns everything else that the state does by force or by statute: it writes and enforces the laws, administers the courts, raises and commands the standing armies, and adjusts — its own preferred word — the structures of imperial life to keep the unipolar order stable. Unlike the Capes, the Bureau admits humans, dwarves, and halflings alike, which makes it the one great imperial institution where the three ruling peoples sit together, and the natural venue for the dwarven and halfling kingdoms to exert the influence their junior-partner status otherwise denies them.

The two factions divide the Empire's coercive power between them: the Bureau commands the law and the army, the Capes command magic and the bodies of magical peoples. They are not natural allies. The Bureau is multi-racial, statutory, and institutional; the Capes are human-only, semi-autonomous, and answerable to no court. Where their jurisdictions touch — an unlicensed caster who is also a fugitive from imperial law, a magical item that is also military materiel, an elf whose correction is also a Bureau matter — the friction between them is one of the central political engines of the setting.
