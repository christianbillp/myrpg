The Fortune Guards are the bankers and lawyers of the halfling Powers, and the wealthiest of the three by a wide margin. They hold deposits, issue loans, underwrite ventures, and command a stable of advocates so skilled that their legal opinion is treated almost as law in itself.

In their everyday dealings they are genuinely pleasant to work with: friendly, scrupulously fair, never overcharging for their services, and famously generous, their name attached to charities and public works across the Empire. A Fortune Guard contract is honest, a Fortune Guard rate is reasonable, and a Fortune Guard's word is good. Borrowing from them is the easiest decision in the world. Repaying them is the only one that matters.

Because in the collection of debt the Fortune Guards are ruthless and entirely without compromise. Blackmail, torture, arranged "accidents", beatings delivered by hired goons, the quiet destruction of a debtor's property and livelihood — these are ordinary tools, applied without malice and without mercy to anyone who fails to pay. As a matter of principle they accept only coin; no barter, no favours, no promises, no other currency will settle a Fortune Guard debt.

And here is the part that should frighten anyone signing their paper: between their wealth, their political influence, and their genuinely superb legal apparatus, the Fortune Guards have never in their entire history been found guilty of a single crime. Not one. Everyone knows what they do. No court has ever been able to prove it.

Among the halfling Powers, the Fortune Guards are the slow, patient, inescapable one. The [[silver-service|Silver Service]] trades in secrets and the [[circus-of-the-moon|Circus]] trades in the hearts of the people, but the Fortune Guards trade in obligation, and an obligation to them does not expire, forgive, or forget. They are perhaps the single most useful and most dangerous institution an adventuring party can deal with: a fair lender who will fund almost anything, and a creditor who will pursue an unpaid debt to the ends of the Empire and remain, throughout, perfectly within the law.

Like its siblings, the Fortune Guards are bound by the [[oath-of-non-competition|Oath of Non-Competition]] — they keep to business and do not stage entertainments or run restaurants. What they sell is debt, and the certainty that it will be repaid. See also the [[halfling-sixer|Halfling Sixer]] proverb, which names the three Powers' joint method of charming a mark into ruin (the Guards play steps 3 and 6 — paying the bill, then walking away clean).
