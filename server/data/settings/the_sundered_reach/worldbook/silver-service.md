The Silver Service runs the finest restaurants in the Empire, a network of extraordinarily extravagant establishments scattered through every major city, each staffed by the most skilled halfling chefs alive and serving the very best the Empire can produce. The prices are ruinous and the discretion absolute, which is exactly why the Service's dining rooms have become the premier venues for political intrigue and deal-making in the whole Empire — business that cannot be conducted anywhere else is conducted over a Silver Service table, in private rooms where no one is listening who has not been paid to listen.

An invitation to dine is understood, by everyone who matters, to be a genuinely dangerous thing: you are as likely to be poisoned over the third course as you are to be offered the opportunity of a lifetime, and frequently the two offers arrive at the same meal. The Service profits either way — from the price of the table, the fee for the discretion, and, when required, the fee for the poison.

The Silver Service is one of the three halfling Powers, bound to the others — the [[circus-of-the-moon|Circus of the Moon]] and the [[fortune-guards|Fortune Guards]] — by the [[oath-of-non-competition|Oath of Non-Competition]]. The Service keeps to food; it does not lend money, and it does not stage entertainments. What it sells is the room, and the silence in the room, and what it knows about everything that has ever been said there.
