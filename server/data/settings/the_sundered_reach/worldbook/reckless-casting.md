Magic in the Sundered Reach is powerful but unstable. Every time the player casts a spell, there is a chance — increasing with spell level and recent casting — that the spell draws attention from the silence the gods left behind. The system is designed to encourage mindful, deliberate casting rather than spamming spells in every encounter.

Each spell cast triggers a **Consequence Check**. The base chance is low for cantrips and 1st-level spells and rises with spell level. The chance also escalates with recent casting: each spell cast within the last hour adds a stacking modifier to the next check. A failed check rolls on one of three severity tables — Minor, Moderate, or Severe — with severity determined by spell level and how badly the check failed.

## Severity guidance

- **Minor.** Flavour-only, brief, no mechanical cost. Reminds the player that something is listening.
- **Moderate.** Temporary mechanical penalty, lost resource, or persistent narrative thread.
- **Severe.** Permanent change, dangerous encounter spawn, or quest-level disruption.

## Minor Consequences (d10)

1. The caster's eyes briefly mirror over with silver, reflecting nothing in the room — bystanders notice.
2. A small personal object (coin, button, charm) crumbles to dry ash in the caster's pocket.
3. The caster's voice goes flat and toneless for the next hour; intimidation and persuasion checks at disadvantage.
4. Faint frost or scorch marks bloom on every metal surface the caster touches for the next ten minutes.
5. The caster forgets one trivial but specific memory — a friend's middle name, the taste of a favourite food.
6. Their shadow detaches by a hand's breadth and lags a half-second behind their movements until they next sleep.
7. Every dog, cat, or domesticated animal within fifty feet flinches away from the caster for an hour.
8. A nosebleed, but the blood is colourless and faintly luminescent for a few seconds before drying normal.
9. The next time the caster speaks, the first word comes out in a language they have never learned.
10. Small written marks near the caster (ink on a page, charcoal on a wall) rearrange themselves into a single unfamiliar glyph.

## Moderate Consequences (d10)

1. The caster ages visibly — a streak of white hair, a new line at the eye — and the change does not reverse.
2. Something on the other side of the silence answers: the caster gains a whispered piece of true information they did not want.
3. A nearby flame, hearth-fire, or torch goes out and cannot be relit for an hour, no matter the fuel.
4. The caster's reflection in any surface is delayed by several seconds for the next day.
5. Roll on the spell list: the caster loses access to one randomly chosen prepared spell until they next complete a long rest in consecrated ground.
6. A small, mundane creature within sight (a rat, a sparrow, a fly) dies instantly and silently.
7. The next door, gate, or container the caster opens reveals a brief glimpse of somewhere else before resolving normally.
8. The caster acquires a phobia they didn't have — of running water, of mirrors, of being touched — that lasts one full day.
9. Their true name is now known to something. The next time they sleep, they hear it spoken in the dark.
10. An item in the caster's inventory becomes subtly wrong — the same item, but from a version of the world that isn't quite this one. It functions, but unsettles anyone who handles it.

## Severe Consequences (d10)

1. Something steps through. A creature of CR equal to the spell's level appears within 30 feet, hostile, and remembers the caster's face.
2. A region of the caster's mind goes dark: they permanently lose one point of Intelligence or Wisdom (player chooses).
3. The spell goes off — and then goes off again, in the same place, twenty-four hours later, with no caster present.
4. A piece of the caster is taken as payment: a finger, an eye, a voice, a name. The loss is permanent until restored by something equivalent.
5. All magic within 60 feet fails for the next ten minutes, including the caster's own. Active spells end. Magic items go inert.
6. The caster is marked. Until the mark is removed (a major quest), every spell they cast has a 50% chance to trigger another consequence roll.
7. The thing on the other side of the silence opens an eye. The caster gains permanent dim vision into the place where the gods used to be — every long rest now requires a Wisdom save against a short-term madness.
8. Time hiccups. The caster loses 1d6 days; they wake somewhere they don't recognise, with no memory of the interval, and the world has moved on without them.
9. A bystander or ally within 30 feet pays the price instead: they drop to 0 HP and stabilise, but a permanent change settles on them (white hair, a scar, a new fear) and they will remember who caused it.
10. The caster's connection to magic ruptures. They cannot cast spells of any kind until they find and complete a binding ritual at one of the old empire's ruined wards — a quest in itself.

## Why this exists

The Consequence system is the in-game expression of the setting's central premise: every spell is a knock on the same door, and the door is what ended the world the first time. Mechanically it discourages spell-spam without forbidding magic; narratively it gives the [[white-capes|White Capes]] a reason to exist (their entire authority rests on the fact that casting is genuinely dangerous) and gives elves, [[quota-and-passage|whose magic is inherent]], a quiet existential horror to live with (they cannot choose not to cast).
