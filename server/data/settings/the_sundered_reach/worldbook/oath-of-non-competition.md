What makes the halfling Powers so formidable is that they do not compete. The three factions are bound to one another by an ancient and absolute Oath of Non-Competition: the [[silver-service|Silver Service]] keeps to food, the [[circus-of-the-moon|Circus]] keeps to art, and the [[fortune-guards|Fortune Guards]] keep to business, and no Power encroaches on another's territory. The Service does not lend money; the Guards do not stage entertainments; the Circus does not broker the deals it enforces. Each stays within its sphere, and within that sphere it is supreme. The result is that the halfling seat behaves not like three rival cartels jockeying for advantage but like a single body with three hands, each doing what it does better than anyone else alive and none of them reaching for what belongs to a sibling.

The Oath is reinforced by how the Powers choose their leaders. A head of the Silver Service is selected for a genuine, consuming passion for food; a head of the Circus for a true devotion to the arts and to the moving of hearts through them; a head of the Fortune Guards for a real love of business and the craft of money. Leadership is not awarded for ambition or for raw cunning — those qualities are abundant and assumed — but for authentic passion for the faction's actual purpose. A halfling who hungers for power rather than for cooking will never lead the Service, because a leader whose heart is elsewhere is precisely the kind of leader who would eventually look across the table at a sibling faction's territory and want it. By selecting for passion, the Powers ensure that each faction is led by someone with no desire to be anything other than what they are — which is the surest possible guarantee that the Oath will hold.

For outsiders this tight-knit unity is the most dangerous fact about the halflings. To borrow from the Fortune Guards is to be known to the Silver Service and reachable by the Circus, because the three share what they learn and honour each other's interests without hesitation. There is no playing one Power against another; the cold war an outsider might hope to exploit does not exist. Cross one halfling Power and, in every way that matters, you have crossed all three.

Between them the three Powers give the halfling seat a reach that neither the Capes' magic nor the Bureau's armies can fully counter: the Silver Service knows everyone's secrets, the Circus owns everyone's hearts, and the Fortune Guards hold everyone's debts — and the three share all of it. The halflings rarely need to threaten openly — like the dwarven guilds, they would rather take the fee than make the threat — but every player in imperial politics understands that a halfling faction can ruin you financially, expose you socially, turn the realm against you, or simply remove you, and sometimes do all of it before breakfast.
