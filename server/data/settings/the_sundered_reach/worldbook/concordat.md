The Concordat is the official body that represents all elves to the Empire, and it is an elven institution only in the sense that its members are elves. Its true purpose is the opposite of what its name suggests: it exists not to advance elven interests but to keep the elves orderly, governable, and — above all — non-rebellious. The Empire has no wish to see the elves exterminated; it needs them. Elven labour is woven through the lower tiers of imperial life, and the hereditary contempt humans hold for the elves coexists quite comfortably with the Empire's reliance on them. A docile, productive elven population is worth far more than a dead one. The Concordat is the instrument that keeps them docile.

Membership is elves-only — no human, dwarf, or halfling may join — which lets the Empire present the Concordat as elven self-government. But its leadership is appointed by the Empire, not elected by the elves, and the Empire chooses its appointees with deliberate cynicism. The ideal Concordat leader is selfish, paranoid, and personally ambitious: precisely the sort of elf who will never rally their people toward rebellion, because their instinct is to protect their own position rather than their kin. Such a leader spends their energy hunting dissidents, informing on agitators, and crushing any stirring of unity before it can threaten the order that keeps them in power. The Empire does not need to oppress the elves directly when it can appoint an elf who will do it from the inside, and take the blame for it too.

The result is a faction that functions as the elves' own secret police. The Concordat maintains its own enforcers, keeps its own watch-lists, and disappears its own troublemakers, all under the legitimising banner of elven representation. It is hated and it is needed in equal measure, and which an elf feels usually depends on what the Concordat has most recently done to them. To some, the Concordat is a collaborationist apparatus of quislings who sell out their own people for scraps of imperial favour — every dissident it betrays is a betrayal of the whole race. To others, it is the only shield the elves have: a buffer that absorbs imperial pressure, negotiates the small mercies, and prevents the [[white-capes|Capes]] from simply dealing with the elves directly and far more brutally. Both views are correct, which is what makes the Concordat so corrosive. It is genuinely the only thing standing between the elves and something worse, and it is genuinely an instrument of their oppression, and it has every incentive to keep both of those things true forever.

The Concordat also runs the operational machinery of the [[quota-and-passage|Quota]] — selecting which elves die in each adjustment, managing the schedule, and ultimately performing the executions with its own hands rather than imperial ones. The leash on the Concordat leadership is total: failure of any adjustment costs them their lives, replaced by a new leadership the Bureau installs to drive the next cull. That is why the Concordat is more ruthless than any human overseer could be — it is killing its own people to stay alive.

**Hooks:** a Concordat leader who needs the party to quietly remove a rival or a dissident; an elf the party sympathises with who is on a Concordat watch-list; a faction of elves trying to build unity in secret, with the Concordat as their most dangerous enemy rather than the Capes; a Concordat appointee who privately wants out, or who has begun to believe their own cover story; the question of who succeeds an aging or murdered Concordat leader, and which masters the candidates truly serve.
