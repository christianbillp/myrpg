Bandits are what the Empire's order produces at its edges. The roads between the great cities are long, the [[bureau-of-adjustment|Bureau]]'s armies are stretched, and the dwindling traffic that the New Empire has not yet absorbed leaves a great deal of land where law is a rumour. Bandit crews are typically small, locally rooted, and short-lived; they may be ex-soldiers, dispossessed villagers, refugees from the interregnum, or simply the desperate. Each crew is its own faction with its own motives — survival, profit, grievance, ideology, or some mix of all four — and is simpler than the great factions because it has to be.

Bandits are a **type** of minor faction, not a single unified power. Specific crews, their leaders, their territories, and their grievances are for the GM to invent as the campaign requires.
