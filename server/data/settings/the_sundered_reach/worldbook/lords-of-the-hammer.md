The Lords of the Hammer are the masters of everything that does not move. Roads, bridges, dams, aqueducts, walls, foundations, tunnels, towers, the buildings of every city in the Empire — if it is meant to stand still and last, the Hammer built it or certified it. Their work is the permanent skeleton of imperial civilisation, and their reputation rests on durability: a Hammer-marked structure is expected to outlive the people who commissioned it, and usually does. They are conservative in method, exacting in standards, and quietly proud that the New Empire physically rests on their work.

Together with the [[masters-of-the-wrench|Masters of the Wrench]], the Hammer guild gives the dwarven kingdom enormous practical leverage: the Empire cannot maintain a road, raise a fortification, mill its grain, or move its goods without dwarven work, which is precisely the structural power that lets a junior partner hold its seat. The guilds rarely use that leverage politically — they would rather take the commission than make the threat — but everyone in the Bureau understands that the leverage is there.

Both guilds are open to all peoples: a human or halfling (or anyone else) with the skill and the patience can be admitted, and the work is judged by the work. This makes them, alongside the Bureau, one of the few genuinely cross-racial institutions in the Empire.
