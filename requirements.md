# MyRPG — Requirements

## Overview

A browser-based 2D single-player RPG built with modern web technologies.

## Functional Requirements and Roadmap

---

### Core Rules

All D&D 5e SRD mechanics: movement, combat, action economy, checks, saves, conditions, and damage rules.

#### Done

- **US-001** As a player, I want to move my character around the world using keyboard input (WASD and arrow keys) so that I can explore the game world. The world is represented as a tile-based map where each tile equals 5 feet, making movement structured and tile-aligned.
- **US-005** As a player, I want to engage in turn-based combat with enemies so that exploration feels challenging and rewarding. Combat is fully integrated into the map: movement (30 ft = 6 tiles per turn) and actions happen on the same map, following SRD 5.2.1 rules (initiative, attack rolls, Greatsword with Savage Attacker and Graze mastery, Second Wind, Nimble Escape, death saving throws). A Critical Hit (natural 20) rolls all damage dice twice including Sneak Attack dice. Making a ranged attack (including thrown weapons) while within 5 feet of an enemy who can see the attacker and does not have the Incapacitated condition imposes Disadvantage on the attack roll. Death Saving Throws follow the full SRD rules: rolling a natural 1 counts as two failures; rolling a natural 20 immediately restores 1 HP and ends the Unconscious condition; taking any damage while at 0 HP causes one automatic failure (two if the damage is from a Critical Hit); accumulating 3 successes makes the character Stable, which stops further death saves without restoring HP. The combat log is a two-column display: the left column shows immersive narrative ("Hit — 8 slashing"), the right column shows dice detail ("d20(14)+5=19 vs AC 14 · 1d6[5]+3"). Each row is colour-coded by outcome. It is possible to scroll through the entire combat log.
- **US-017** As a developer, I want every creature's stat block to be sourced directly from the D&D 5e SRD 5.2.1 so that combat mechanics are correct and new creatures can be added without guesswork. Monsters (`MonsterDef`) hold SRD stat blocks: creature type, HP dice formula, full ability scores, an explicit `initiativeBonus` field, and attack entries with attack type (melee / ranged / both), reach, and range. NPCs (`NPCDef`) are a separate type that references a `monsterClass` for their stat block while adding identity fields (name, colour, persona). The combat system selects the correct attack type based on range. Monster roster: Goblin Minion, Bandit, Commoner, Skeleton (CR 1/4, AC 14, bludgeoning vulnerability, Shortsword + Shortbow).
- **US-019** As a player, I want to move around on a randomly generated map so that I can experience a variety of challenges. Each encounter generates a new square map with a random side length between 10 and 30 tiles, where 20–40% of tiles are impassable walls. All passable tiles are guaranteed to be reachable from each other (flood-fill validated). Player and enemies spawn on passable tiles separated by at least 5 tiles. Wall tiles are visually distinct. Movement respects walls including the SRD diagonal corner rule (diagonal movement is blocked when both orthogonal neighbours are walls). The enemy AI uses BFS pathfinding to navigate around walls.
- **US-024** As a player, I want to fight multiple enemies in a single combat encounter so that battles feel more strategic. Simple Combat spawns 2–4 enemies chosen randomly from available enemy types (Goblin Minion and Bandit). All map enemies join combat the moment one triggers it. Enemies are labeled A, B, C… and allies are labeled a, b, c… on the map and in the Turn Order Bar at the top of the Game Map. All non-neutral NPCs (enemies and allies) act sequentially during the NPC phase; enemies attack the player and allies attack the nearest enemy. The active chip in the Turn Order Bar is highlighted green and dead chips are dimmed. Combat ends only when all enemy-disposition NPCs are defeated; surviving allies revert to neutral disposition.
- **US-054** As a player, I want NPCs to have a disposition (ally, neutral, or enemy) so that the same entity can shift allegiance during play without losing its identity. All creatures — enemies, allies, and social NPCs — share a single `NpcState` type differentiated by `disposition: 'ally' | 'neutral' | 'enemy'`. Enemy-disposition NPCs attack the player in combat; ally-disposition NPCs join the initiative order and automatically attack the nearest enemy on their turn; neutral-disposition NPCs stand in the world and do not participate in combat. Rendering is disposition-aware: all three dispositions show an HP bar overlaid at the top of the token circle when damaged — red for enemies, green for allies, blue-grey for neutrals. Neutral NPCs also show their name just above the token. Enemy NPCs receive uppercase labels (A, B, C…) at spawn; ally NPCs receive lowercase labels (a, b, c…) when combat starts or when their disposition changes to ally. Token disposition is synced from server state on every tick so label and HP bar always reflect the current role. When combat ends, surviving allies revert to neutral; enemies are removed from the map. The AIDM can change any NPC's disposition at any time via `set_disposition`.
- **US-055** As a player, I can attack a neutral NPC outside of combat to start a fight, so that I have agency over when and against whom combat begins. Selecting a living non-ally NPC and pressing ATTACK during the exploring phase promotes that NPC to enemy disposition, triggers faction aggro, and then starts combat via a normal initiative roll. Faction aggro: every NPC carries a `factionId` (defaults to its definition id, so all instances of the same NPC type share a faction); when any NPC turns hostile all neutral NPCs sharing that `factionId` immediately join as enemies before initiative is rolled. Faction aggro fires from both player-initiated attacks and AIDM `set_disposition` calls, ensuring the mechanic is consistent regardless of how hostility starts.
- **US-025** As a player, I want the action economy to follow D&D 5e SRD rules so that combat feels authentic. Each player turn grants one Action and one Bonus Action. Attack consumes the Action; Second Wind (Fighter) and Hide via Cunning Action (Rogue) consume the Bonus Action. Drinking a Health Potion costs the Bonus Action in combat and is free during exploration. The player remains in their turn after spending either resource and must press End Turn to hand initiative to the enemies. The Phase Text appends "· action used" or "· bonus used" when the respective resource is spent; buttons that require a spent resource are dimmed and disabled.
- **US-042** As a player, I want Dash, Dodge, and Disengage actions so that I have tactical options beyond attacking. **Dash**: spend Action to gain extra movement equal to Speed for the turn. **Dodge**: until start of next turn, attacks against the player have Disadvantage and the player has Advantage on DEX saves; shown in Phase Text. **Disengage**: movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn. These appear as Action Buttons in the Player Panel when the Action has not yet been spent.
- **US-043** As a developer, I want a formal Advantage/Disadvantage system so that all D20 Test modifiers are consistent and composable. Any D20 Test can be tagged with Advantage or Disadvantage sources. When resolving the roll: Advantage → roll 2d20, take higher; Disadvantage → take lower; both present → cancel, roll normally. Sources include: player hidden (Advantage on attack), Vex mastery (Advantage on attacker's next attack roll against the same target), Dodge (Disadvantage on attacks against dodger), Surprise (Disadvantage on surprised creature's Initiative), ranged attack while adjacent to a conscious enemy (Disadvantage). The combat log shows both dice values when the mechanic applies.
- **US-041** As a player, I want enemies to make Opportunity Attacks when I move out of their reach, and I want to make one when an enemy moves away from me, so that positioning carries meaningful risk. When a creature uses its movement to leave the reach of an enemy that can see it, that enemy may spend its Reaction to make one melee attack. Each creature has one Reaction per round, reset at the start of its turn. The player's Opportunity Attack fires automatically when an enemy moves away. Enemy Opportunity Attacks fire as the player moves through a threatened tile. The combat log notes each Opportunity Attack.
- **US-047** As a developer, I want Passive Perception implemented so that hiding is not an automatic success. Passive Perception = 10 + Perception modifier. A player's Hide check must exceed the highest Passive Perception among all living enemies to succeed; failure is logged and the player remains visible.
- **US-049** As a developer, I want Massive Damage / Instant Death implemented. When a hit reduces the player to 0 HP and leftover damage ≥ player's HP maximum, the player dies instantly without death saving throws. The combat log notes the instant death.
- **US-053** As a developer, I want monster Resistance, Vulnerability, and Immunity modeled so that damage is applied correctly per SRD stat blocks. Each `MonsterDef` carries optional `resistances`, `vulnerabilities`, and `immunities` arrays of damage types, plus an optional `conditionImmunities` array. Player attacks check the target's lists and halve (resistant), double (vulnerable), or negate entirely (immune) damage before applying to HP, with a log entry noting the modifier. Condition immunity prevents the condition from being applied at all. Immunity takes precedence over vulnerability and resistance. Each `PlayerAttack` carries a `damageType` field sourced from the equipped weapon (or "bludgeoning" for Unarmed Strike). The Skeleton is immune to Poison damage and the Poisoned condition.
- **US-046** As a player, I want to take a Short Rest after an encounter so that I can recover HP between fights by spending Hit Dice. A SHORT REST button appears in the Player Panel during the exploring phase when the player is below max HP and has Hit Dice remaining. Each click spends one Hit Die (d10+CON for Fighter, d8+CON for Rogue) and heals that amount, logged in the Combat Log. Hit Dice equal the character level; they reset at the start of every new encounter (Long Rest equivalent).
- **US-034** As a player, I want to throw inventory items as ranged attacks so that I have a ranged option when no adjacent enemy is present. Proper thrown weapons (javelin, dagger) use their weapon stats, mastery, and proficiency bonus; all other items are improvised (1d4 bludgeoning, STR mod, no proficiency bonus). Each weapon JSON carries `thrown`, `throwNormal`, and `throwLong` fields (ft); improvised range is 20/60 ft. A THROW… button appears in the Player Panel when the action has not been spent, no enemy is adjacent, and at least one throwable item can reach a living enemy; pressing it opens an inline picker listing qualifying items. Item range per-item: javelin 30/120 ft, dagger 20/60 ft, improvised 20/60 ft. On a hit, the thrown item enters the target's inventory and is dropped to the map at the target's tile when the creature is killed, allowing it to be recovered. On a miss, the item is placed directly on the map at the target's tile. The AIDM can also trigger throws (including map items) via the `throw_item` tool.
- **US-035** As a developer, I want all SRD weapon mastery properties implemented so that weapons behave correctly. All four masteries used by player weapons are now implemented: **Graze** (miss still deals STR-mod damage of the weapon's type), **Vex** (hit gives the attacker Advantage on their next attack roll against that creature before the end of the attacker's next turn), **Sap** (hit gives the target Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of the attacker's next turn), **Slow** (hit reduces target speed by 10 ft until the start of the attacker's next turn). Each is resolved in `CombatSystem.ts` and wired into the combat log.
- **US-051** As a developer, I want a formal conditions framework so that status effects are consistent, visible, and extensible. Conditions are tracked as a set per creature and their mechanical effects are applied at resolution time by the combat and movement systems. All condition logic lives in `server/src/engine/ConditionSystem.ts` and applies equally to all creature types — player, ally, and enemy. The Target Panel shows active conditions on the selected creature in amber. The AIDM can apply and remove conditions via `apply_condition` and `remove_condition` tools; exhaustion is set via `set_exhaustion_level`. The mechanical effects of each implemented condition are: **Blinded** — own attack rolls have Disadvantage; attack rolls against have Advantage. **Frightened** — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. **Grappled** — Speed 0; own attack rolls have Disadvantage. **Incapacitated** — can't take Actions, Bonus Actions, or Reactions (prevents Opportunity Attacks); applies to all creatures. **Invisible** — own attack rolls have Advantage; attack rolls against have Disadvantage. **Paralyzed** — has the Incapacitated condition; Speed 0; attack rolls against have Advantage; melee hits from within 5 ft are Critical Hits (applies to melee and thrown attacks); auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Poisoned** — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. **Prone** — Disadvantage on own attack rolls; melee attacks against have Advantage, ranged attacks against have Disadvantage; costs half Speed (round down) to stand up, which happens automatically at the start of the creature's turn. **Restrained** — Speed 0; own attack rolls have Disadvantage; attack rolls against have Advantage; Disadvantage on Dex saves. **Stunned** — has the Incapacitated condition; attack rolls against have Advantage; auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Unconscious** — has the Incapacitated condition; auto-applies Prone; Speed 0; attack rolls against have Advantage; melee hits from within 5 ft are Critical Hits; auto-fail Str/Dex saves. **Exhaustion** (numeric level 0–5) — each level imposes −2 to all D20 Tests (ability checks and saving throws); level 5 is lethal. **Vexed** (custom) — own attack rolls have Disadvantage; applied by Vex and Sap weapon masteries; lasts until end of next turn. **Slowed** (custom) — Speed reduced by 10 ft; applied by Slow mastery; lasts until end of next turn.
- **US-037** As a player, I want my full skill list stored and used in play so that ability checks reflect my character's actual proficiencies and create meaningful risk. Each character's `skills` map (stored in the character JSON and persisted in `SaveData`) holds all 18 SRD skills keyed by camelCase name (e.g. `sleightOfHand`). Each value is the pre-computed total modifier: ability modifier plus proficiency bonus once for proficiency, twice for expertise, or not at all if untrained. The dedicated `perceptionBonus` and `stealthBonus` fields on `PlayerDef` are replaced by the general map. Skills are surfaced to the AIDM on every message. When the player asks to do something that does not automatically succeed, the AIDM calls a `request_ability_check` tool specifying the skill, a DC (Very Easy 5 / Easy 10 / Medium 15 / Hard 20 / Very Hard 25 per SRD), and a reason. The server rolls d20 + the skill modifier and returns the result; the AIDM overlay injects it as a distinct 🎲 roll entry (green on success, red on failure) between the player's message and the DM's narrative response. The AIDM then narrates success or failure and uses its other tools to apply consequences — awarding or deducting HP, XP, or gold, moving entities, or altering the encounter — as the fiction demands.
- **US-036** As a player, I want saving throws tracked for all creatures so that the AIDM can call for saves when the story demands it. Every creature carries a `savingThrows` map of all six modifiers: character JSONs derive these at load time from `savingThrowProficiencies` (ability modifier + proficiency bonus if proficient); monster JSONs store the values directly from the SRD stat block SAVE column. All six player saving throws are surfaced to the AIDM on every message. The AIDM actively calls for saving throws — against traps, spells, environmental hazards, or any narrative moment where a save is appropriate — using the same `request_ability_check` flow as skill checks, rolling d20 + the relevant save modifier, then narrating success or failure and applying consequences via its other tools.

#### Next

- **US-058** As a developer, I want the remaining SRD conditions fully enforced so that condition effects match the Rules Glossary. The following effects are not yet implemented and require architectural work: **Frightened** — the source-tracking sub-rule that prevents the frightened creature from moving closer to the source of its fear (requires a `sourceId` on the condition entry); **Charmed** — the sub-rule preventing the charmed creature from attacking or targeting the charmer with harmful abilities (requires `sourceId`); **Grappled** — the SRD rule that the creature can't move the grappler imposes no engine penalty on the grappler (requires `sourceId` to distinguish); **Blinded** — the sub-rule that a blinded creature automatically fails ability checks that rely on sight is narrative-only in the current engine; **Deafened** — the condition exists in the SRD but has no mechanical effect in the current turn-based engine; **Petrified** — full treatment (object immune, advantage/auto-crit against, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, immunity to poison/disease accrual) requires a sub-type flag on the NpcState; **Invisible** — NPC invisibility currently applies attack advantage/disadvantage for the player-vs-enemy flow, but NPCs with invisible targeting other NPCs (ally-vs-enemy) are not yet handled. All source-dependent effects share the same design decision: add an optional `sourceId: string` field to condition entries (stored as `{ name: string; sourceId?: string }` rather than plain strings) so the engine can check the relationship between the conditioned creature and its source.
- **US-057** As a player, I want the six remaining SRD core actions available so that I have a complete action vocabulary and the engine matches the SRD action table. **Help**: spend Action to grant Advantage on an ally NPC's next attack roll, or to stabilise an Unconscious creature with a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check. **Influence**: spend Action to make a Charisma (Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion) or Wisdom (Animal Handling) check against a selected NPC; the roll is resolved via `request_ability_check` and the AIDM applies the attitude change using `set_disposition`. An INFLUENCE button appears in the Player Panel when the action has not been spent and a neutral NPC is selected. **Study**: spend Action to make an Intelligence (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, or Religion) check; resolved via `request_ability_check`. A STUDY button appears in the Player Panel when the action has not been spent. **Utilize**: spend Action to interact with a nonmagical object in the environment; fires an AIDM message describing what the player attempts, and the AIDM resolves the outcome narratively using whatever tools apply. A UTILIZE button appears in the Player Panel when the action has not been spent. **Ready**: spend Action to declare a trigger condition and a response action or attack; when the trigger fires before the start of the player's next turn the Reaction is consumed to enact the readied action. Requires a reaction/interrupt system distinct from the existing turn-order flow. **Magic**: spend Action to cast a spell using a spell slot (requires spellcasting class characters, a spell list, slot tracking, and concentration rules — a dedicated system separate from this requirement).
- **US-044** As a player, I want some map tiles to be Difficult Terrain so that terrain shapes movement. Difficult Terrain costs 2 ft per foot moved. Map files and the generator can designate difficult terrain tiles. Movement highlights account for terrain cost.
- **US-045** As a player, I want cover to protect targets near walls so that positioning near obstacles is tactically meaningful. Half Cover (+2 AC and DEX saves), Three-Quarters Cover (+5 AC and DEX saves), Total Cover (untargetable). Cover is determined geometrically at attack resolution.
- **US-048** As a player, I want Surprise to apply when combat starts unexpectedly. Enemies that are unaware when combat begins (e.g. player is hidden) roll Initiative with Disadvantage. If an ambush catches the player unaware, the player rolls Initiative with Disadvantage. The combat log notes surprised combatants.
- **US-050** As a player, I want a Shove option so that I can reposition or knock down enemies. Shove is an Unarmed Strike option that consumes the Action. The target (no more than one size larger than the player) makes a STR or DEX saving throw of their choice (DC = 8 + player STR mod + proficiency bonus). On a failed save the player chooses the effect: either push the target 5 feet to an adjacent passable tile, or give the target the Prone condition. A creature can't be shoved into a wall or occupied tile; the nearest valid tile is used instead.
- **US-052** As a player, I want to knock enemies unconscious instead of killing them so that I can resolve encounters non-lethally. When a melee attack would reduce an enemy to 0 HP, the player can choose "Knock Out". The creature is reduced to 1 HP and given the Unconscious condition (Incapacitated, Prone, Speed 0, auto-fail STR and DEX saves, Advantage on attacks against it, melee hits within 5 ft are Critical Hits). It is removed from the initiative order and remains Unconscious until it regains any HP or until a character spends an Action to administer first aid (DC 10 Wisdom Medicine check).
- As a developer, I want stabilizing implemented so that an unconscious character can be saved without a healing item. A character with 0 HP that accumulates 3 death save successes becomes Stable and stops making death saves without regaining HP. Any character can also stabilize an unconscious creature by spending their Action on a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check. A Stable creature retains the Unconscious condition until it regains any HP.
- As a developer, I want Temporary Hit Points modeled so that abilities and items that grant them work correctly. Temp HP act as a buffer before real HP: incoming damage depletes them first, with any overflow carrying into real HP. Temp HP do not stack — when a new source grants them, the creature keeps whichever total is higher. They are lost at the end of a Long Rest. The combat log distinguishes temp HP loss from real HP loss.
- As a player, I want to use part of my movement before and part after my action on the same turn, so that I can move into range, attack, and then reposition. The turn system must support drawing from the movement pool freely before and after the action rather than requiring all movement to occur at the start or end of the turn.

### Content & World

Characters, items, maps, quests, NPCs, save/load, and the content API.

#### Done

- **US-002** As a player, I want to choose between Aldric Vane (Human Fighter) and Miriel Duskwhisper (Wood Elf Rogue) before the encounter begins so that I can experience different combat styles. Each character has distinct mechanics: Aldric uses Greatsword with Savage Attacker and Graze mastery plus Second Wind; Miriel uses Shortsword with Sneak Attack (from hiding), Vex mastery, and a Hide action.
- **US-023** As a player, I want to play on hand-crafted saved maps so that I can experience specific layouts. Seven maps are available: Arena, Dungeon, Ruins, Catacombs, Forest Trail, The Rusty Flagon (tavern), and Narrow Bridge, stored as JSON in `server/data/maps/` and served via `GET /maps`. Each map is pre-processed server-side into a `passable` boolean grid. Map selection is implicit in the premade encounter choice (each `EncounterDef` carries a `mapId`); the standalone map picker overlay is no longer used.
- **US-026** As a developer, I want all game content served from the Fastify REST API so that content can be updated centrally without redeploying the client. Static JSON files live under `server/data/` in per-type subdirectories. The server exposes `GET /characters`, `GET /monsters`, `GET /npcs`, `GET /equipment`, `GET /feats`, `GET /backgrounds`, `GET /species`, `GET /maps`, and `GET /encounters`; each endpoint reads all `.json` files in its directory and returns them as an array. The client preloads all nine collections in `BootScene` and stores them in the Phaser registry.
- **US-027** As a developer, I want NPCs to be a distinct data type from monsters so that non-combat characters can carry dialogue personas without polluting SRD stat blocks. NPCs live in `server/data/npcs/` as `NPCDef` objects with `id`, `name`, `monsterClass` (reference to a `MonsterDef` id for stat resolution), `color`, and an optional `persona` string. At spawn time the client merges the NPC's identity over the referenced monster's stat block. The `POST /npc/chat` endpoint looks up the NPC by id, resolves its persona, and forwards the conversation to the Claude API. Current roster: Wandering Sage (`wanderer`), Local Villager (`villager`), Bram Holdfast the tavern keeper (`tavern_keeper`), Bandit (`bandit_npc`), Frightened Traveller (`commoner`).
- **US-006** As a player, I want to pick up, manage, and use items in an inventory so that I can equip myself for challenges. Up to 3 Health Potions (2d4+2 healing per SRD) spawn as green diamond tokens on the map each encounter; walking onto a token picks it up automatically. XP is recorded in the combat log on each kill. Gold is not automatically awarded — it must be given by the AIDM via the `award_gold` tool (e.g. as quest rewards or loot narrated in the DM overlay). Current GP is shown in the Inventory Overlay stats bar.
- **US-007** As a player, I want to receive and track quests so that I have clear goals and a sense of progression. Quests are assigned automatically at encounter start based on the active encounter types. Simple Combat assigns: First Blood (slay 1 enemy, +10 XP +5 GP), Slay All (slay every enemy spawned, +25 XP +15 GP), and Treasure Hunt (collect 2 Health Potions, +10 XP +5 GP). Exploration assigns: Keen Eye (find 2 secrets, +15 XP +10 GP). Social Interaction assigns: Make Contact (open a conversation with any NPC, +10 XP +5 GP, awarded on the first AI reply or riddle answer). The Player Panel shows a QUESTS section listing each quest as "· Title N/M" while in progress and "✓ Title" when complete. Quest rewards are added to XP and GP and logged in the Combat Log on completion.
- **US-008** As a player, I want my game state saved and loaded automatically so that I can continue where I left off. Save state is split into two files on the server: a **character save** at `server/data/saves/{characterId}.json` (HP, XP, gold, inventory, equipment, Second Wind uses) and a **world save** at `server/data/saves/world.json` (full session/encounter state, referencing the character by `defId`). On boot, `BootScene` calls `GET /world`; if a world save exists the session is restored and `GameScene` opens directly, otherwise `EncounterSetupScene` is shown. When the player reloads the browser and reconnects to an active session, the Introduction Overlay is suppressed — play resumes directly from the saved state. The character save is written on every action and after every AIDM exchange. The world save is deleted when the player presses NEW ENCOUNTER. Each character card in the setup screen shows HP / XP / GP and equipped gear from the character save; a DELETE SAVE button clears the character save without leaving the setup screen.
- **US-056** As a developer, I want feats, backgrounds, and species from SRD 5.2.1 chapter 04–05 stored as structured data objects so that character definitions reference them by ID rather than duplicating their mechanical effects. All 17 feats (`server/data/feats/`), 4 backgrounds (`server/data/backgrounds/`), and 9 species (`server/data/species/`) are implemented as JSON files. Each feat has a `category`, `prerequisites`, `description`, and a structured `effects` object keyed by named mechanical properties. Each background records its ability scores, granted feat, skill and tool proficiencies, and two equipment options. Each species records creature type, size, speed, and an array of traits — each trait carries a `description` and a structured `effects` object. Characters reference these by `speciesId`/`speciesLineage`, `backgroundId`, and `featIds[]`. Two mechanical effects are currently wired into the engine: `applyFeats` derives `savageAttacker` and `fightingStyleDefense` from `featIds`; `applySpecies` derives `speed` from the species base speed plus any lineage speed bonus. All three collections are served via REST (`GET /feats`, `GET /backgrounds`, `GET /species`) and cached in the client registry.
- **US-030** ~~Superseded.~~ Passive NPCs removed. All NPCs are the same type; each encounter specifies zero or more NPCs via the `npcIds` array. Multiple NPCs of the same type (e.g. several villagers) can be included by repeating the same id.

### User Interface

All HTML DOM panels, overlays, HUD, and responsive layout.

#### Done

- **US-040** As a player, I can get a user interface that automatically fits the available space, so I can play the game on different browser window sizes. Phaser's Scale Manager runs in `FIT` mode with `CENTER_BOTH`: the canvas renders at a fixed logical resolution (1900 × 880) and is CSS-scaled to fill the browser window while preserving the aspect ratio. All UI panels and overlays (Player Panel, Target Panel, HUD, Turn Order Bar, and all overlays) are HTML DOM elements positioned and scaled over the canvas by `UIScale` (`client/src/ui/UIScale.ts`), which uses a single `transform: scale()` per panel derived from the canvas bounding rect. A `ResizeObserver` on the canvas re-positions all panels whenever the display size changes. The game world (tiles, tokens, sprites) remains on the Phaser canvas.
- **US-029** As a player, I can select from a list of premade encounters so that I can replay encounters I enjoy. The Encounter Setup Scene shows a two-column layout: Character (left) and Premade Encounter (right — a 2×4 grid of encounter cards). Each card shows the map name, title, encounter-type chips (colour-coded: Combat red, Exploration green, Social blue), and a flavour description. Premade encounters are JSON files in `server/data/encounters/` served via `GET /encounters`; each entry carries `mapId`, `encounterTypes`, and optional fields: `npcIds` (NPCs to spawn), `allyIds` (NPCs spawned with ally disposition near the player), `customIntroduction` (overrides the auto-generated introduction text), `customContext` (overrides the auto-generated AIDM context), and `startingZones` (a visual string array matching map dimensions that designates spawn regions: `P` = player, `A` = allies, `N` = neutral NPCs, `E` = enemies; `#`, `.`, and space are undesignated). Nine encounters ship: Arena Brawl, Dungeon Crawl, Ruins Mystery, Catacomb Hunt, The Sage's Counsel, Full Assault, Forest Ambush, A Night at the Flagon, Bridge Standoff. The BEGIN ENCOUNTER button activates once a character and an encounter are both selected.
- **US-020** As a player, I want to be presented with an encounter setup menu before the game starts so that I can choose my character and encounter. Superseded by US-029; the setup scene now shows a two-column layout (Character | Premade Encounter) rather than the original three-column free-form selector.
- **US-028** As a player, I get an introduction to the encounter when I start it so that I feel more immersed in it. When the game map loads, an Introduction Overlay appears before the player can act. It shows the active encounter type chips, the player's name and class, and a narrative introduction paragraph. ### AI Dungeon Master

The AIDM system, DM tools, and AI-driven encounter types.

#### Done

- **US-004** As a player, I want to select an AI Dialogue encounter so that I can have a freeform conversation with an AI-powered NPC. Superseded by US-021; the `ai_dialogue` encounter type has been removed and merged into `social_interaction`. All NPC encounters now use AI dialogue by default with riddle fallback.
- **US-031** As a player, I can open a conversation with an AI dungeon master (AIDM) at any time during an encounter so that I can interact with the world through free-form text. A persistent "DUNGEON MASTER" button in the HUD opens a full-screen chat overlay powered by Claude Sonnet. The AIDM receives complete, real-time encounter state on every message: player stats (HP, XP, gold, AC, inventory, hidden status, action/bonus used, moves left, Second Wind charges), all combatants (enemies and allies: HP, AC, position, active-turn flag, vexed/hidden status, disposition), all neutral NPCs (name, id, position), quest progress, items on the ground, secrets remaining, the selected target, and the last 20 combat log entries. The AIDM can control all aspects of the encounter via tool use: adjust any entity's HP (`adjust_npc_hp`: entity format `"player"`, `"enemy_A"`, `"ally_a"`, or `"npc_[id]"`; positive delta heals, negative damages; kills the entity when HP reaches 0), award XP/gold, add narrative log entries, teleport entities (player, enemies by uppercase label, allies by lowercase label, neutral NPCs by id), give the player items, remove items from inventory, start or end combat, force-complete quests, toggle player stealth, spawn a new enemy onto the map, despawn an NPC, change any NPC's disposition (`set_disposition`: ally / neutral / enemy; allies automatically receive a lowercase label), apply or remove conditions, and throw any inventory or map item at an enemy (`throw_item`: proper thrown weapons use weapon stats; other items are improvised 1d4 bludgeoning). The selected target is surfaced to the AIDM with its entity id both in the focus line and the NPC list. When `trigger_combat` is called in a social encounter with no enemies, all present NPCs are converted to enemy combatants in-place (same position and stats), cleared from the NPC roster, and combat begins. When moving multiple entities to the same tile, each is placed on the nearest free passable tile that is not already occupied by any other entity. The AIDM roleplays all NPCs in their own voice, constrained by their persona and knowledge. When the DM overlay is re-opened after the player has taken actions, new combat log entries since the last conversation are automatically injected as a context update so the DM is always current. Pronouns ("them", "it") are resolved to the player's currently selected target. Conversation history is stored server-side in the session and preserved across open/close cycles for the duration of the encounter; the client retains a local copy only for display. The system prompt is split into a static instructions section (eligible for Anthropic prompt caching) and a dynamic state section injected into each user message, so the cached portion is never rebuilt between turns. Historical `[CURRENT STATE]` blocks are stripped from the conversation history before it is sent to the model — only the player's plain text messages are retained in history, while fresh state is always injected with the current message. The DM operates under two additional constraints: **WORLD GROUNDING** (only references creatures, items, and events present in the current state or established in the conversation — never invents NPCs, companions, or off-screen events, and does not assert physical details beyond what is tracked in game state, i.e. HP and conditions); **TURN ORDER** (during `player_turn`, does not narrate or simulate enemy turns — the combat engine resolves them automatically when the player ends their turn). Gold transactions require the `award_gold` tool before narration — the DM never narrates gold as received or spent without the tool confirming it.
- **US-021** As a player, I want to select a Social Interaction encounter so that I can engage with an NPC rather than fight. All creature communication — with NPCs and enemies alike — goes through the Dungeon Master overlay (Claude Sonnet). The player opens the DM overlay and addresses any creature by name or pronoun; the DM responds in that creature's voice, constrained by its persona and knowledge. Enemies may taunt, plead, or bargain. The DM calls `complete_quest('make_contact')` automatically when the player first successfully engages an NPC in a social encounter.
- **US-022** As a player, I want to select the Exploration encounter type so that I can discover hidden secrets on the map. When active, 4 secrets are placed on the map (invisible to the player). A SEARCH button appears in the Player Panel during exploration while secrets remain. The HUD phase text appends "· search available" as a hint. Using Search performs a Wisdom (Perception) check (d20 + perception bonus vs secret DC 10/12/15). While adjacent to a secret tile, success reveals the reward (gold, a Health Potion, or a lore inscription) and logs it; failure produces a flavour observation. Each secret can only be found once. The SEARCH button is hidden when no secrets remain.
- **US-039** As a player, I can choose between multiple AIDM personas so that I can tailor the game to my mood. Two personas are available, toggled via STORY / DEV chips in the DM overlay header. **Story DM** (default): enforces SRD 5.2.1 rules and in-world logic. It operates on a tool-first rule: every game effect it narrates must be enacted via the corresponding tool before it is described — weapon throws call `throw_item`, damage to any entity calls `adjust_npc_hp`, movement calls `move_entity`, and so on. If an effect cannot be enacted with the available tools, the DM does not narrate it as happening. It explicitly rejects requests that would require conjuring items or creatures from nothing — `add_item` and `spawn_enemy` are only used when the fiction justifies it (finding loot, triggering an ambush), never simply because the player asks. It redirects rejected requests toward a realistic in-world alternative. It never uses meta phrases about dice mechanics — roll results appear via the 🎲 overlay entry and the DM narrates only the in-world outcome. **Dev DM**: intended for development and testing — fulfils all requests without restriction, using any tool needed, and replies with brief mechanical feedback (which tools were called and what changed) rather than narrative prose. The selected persona is sent to the server on every message and persists across open/close cycles for the duration of the encounter.

### Story Progression

Character equipment, save persistence, levelling, and long-term adventure structure.

#### Done

- **US-032** As a player, I want to find and equip gear with stats so that my equipment choices meaningfully affect gameplay. Each character has a default loadout sourced from their character sheet (Aldric: Chain Mail + Greatsword equipped, Flail + 8 Javelins carried; Miriel: Leather Armor + Shortsword equipped, 4 Daggers + Shortbow carried). Equipment is split into three slot types — armor, weapon, offhand (shield) — stored in `EquipmentSlots`. Armor AC is computed per SRD category (light: base+DEX; medium: base+min(DEX,2); heavy: fixed base) plus +1 for Fighting Style: Defense and +2 for a shield. Finesse weapons use the higher of STR/DEX for attack and damage. Character stats that depend on data objects are never stored in character JSON files — they are always computed at startup and session creation by three functions that run in order: (1) `applySpecies` reads `speciesId` and `speciesLineage` to derive `speed` (e.g. Wood Elf: 30 base + 5 lineage = 35); (2) `applyFeats` reads `featIds` to derive `savageAttacker` and `fightingStyleDefense`; (3) `applyEquipment` reads `defaultEquipment` to derive `ac` and `mainAttack`. An INVENTORY button in the HUD opens the Inventory Overlay, which shows the three equipped slots with computed stats and UNEQUIP buttons, a scrollable carried-items list with EQUIP buttons (identical items grouped with a ×N count), and a live AC + attack summary bar. Equipping a two-handed weapon auto-unequips any held shield. Equipping one item from a stack removes only that instance. The Player Panel AC stat updates live whenever gear changes. Equipped items are persisted in the save file. The AIDM is aware of equipped armor, weapon, and shield and can grant or remove items via `add_item` and `remove_item`.
- **US-033** As a player, I want to see my save information inside the character selection card so that I know where each character left off. Each character card in the Encounter Setup Scene shows HP / XP / GP from the saved state and the currently equipped weapon and armor. A DELETE SAVE button inside the card clears the save from localStorage and the server without leaving the setup screen. On load, each card always syncs from the server, updating HP/XP/GP, equipped gear, encounter log, and story log in-place when the data arrives. The last-played character's card is pre-selected on boot.
- **US-059** As a player, I can read a Story Log which gives me an AI-generated prose recap of every encounter I have completed, so that I can build a narrative record of my character's adventures. A STORY LOG button on the character card in the Encounter Setup Scene opens the Story Log Overlay. The overlay lists all completed encounters in chronological order; each entry shows the date, encounter type, and a short prose narrative generated by Claude Sonnet. The narrative is written in third-person, two to three sentences long (one short paragraph), weaves the encounter description naturally into the story, translates mechanical events into fiction (hits become blows landed, kills become deaths), includes meaningful dialogue in italics, and omits all mechanical numbers. Narratives are generated on demand via `POST /save/:characterId/storylog`, which processes only encounters not yet in the log and appends new entries — old entries are never rewritten. A GENERATE N ENTRIES button in the overlay triggers generation and is disabled when the log is already up to date. A REWRITE ALL debug button regenerates all entries from scratch. Generated narratives are stored in the character save under `storylog` and persist across sessions. The generation logic lives in `server/src/storylog.ts`.
